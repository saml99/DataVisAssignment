Waste Collection in Australia

Trajectory of Waste around Australia

The visualisation to the left depicts the trajectory of waste items that were collected at varying sites during Clean Up Australia day over the years of 2014 to 2019. Clean up Australia Day aims to inspire communities to clean up, fix up and conserve the environment, yet what this visualisation represents is the current trajectory of waste being collected each year as well as the disappointing fact that waste continues to be as high if not higher than the previous year. The buttons below can be used collapse and show the multiple site types which Clean Up Australia attend, in order to compare the trajectory and amount each site collects for any given year. User's are also encouraged to hover their cursor over the varying points on the line in order to display the accurate total for the year.

*Other refers to any non-identifiable sites, meaning it did not match an established site*

Size and Impact of Waste around Australia

The visualisation to the left depicts the overall size and impact of waste around Australia that was collected at varying sites during Clean Up Australia Day over the years of 2014 to 2019. Clean Up Australia Day aims to inspire communities to clean up, fix up and conserve the environment, yet this visualisation represents the scale of which waste is being collected each year as well as the devastating total of waste that can be collected over one day over four years. The User is encouraged to hover their cursor over the individual colours in the bars to display the exact number of waste collected at the specific site for the specific year.

*Other refers to any non-identifiable sites, meaning it did not match an established site*
